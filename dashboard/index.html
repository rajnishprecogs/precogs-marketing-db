✉️ Email Sequence Architecture

Each sequence = 4 emails over 12 days. Always plain text, <80 words, one soft CTA.

Day 0
Pain + Curiosity
Lead with THEIR pain. Ask a question. No links, no pitch.
"What's your team's false positive rate on code scans right now?"
→
Day 3
Social Proof + Insight
Share a data point or unique capability. Build credibility.
"We're the only platform catching PII before it's sent to AI assistants."
→
Day 7
Value Bomb
Give something useful regardless of whether they buy. CVE analysis, case study.
"CVE-2026-XXXXX affects Django apps — most SAST tools miss it. Here's our analysis."
→
Day 12
Breakup
Short, no pressure. Creates FOMO. Highest reply-rate email.
"If this isn't a priority right now, I'll close this thread. Just hit reply if it is."

Sequence A: False Positive Fatigue

For CISOs / Security Leaders

Subject: {{firstName}}, quick question on {{company}}'s appsec
Hook: "What's your false positive rate? We keep hearing 30-40%. Precogs runs at <2%."
CTA: "Worth a 15-min chat?"

Sequence B: AI Code Security

For VP Eng / CTO

Subject: {{company}} + AI-generated code security
Hook: "34% of Copilot-generated functions have OWASP Top 10 vulns."
CTA: "Worth a quick look?"

Sequence C: Competitor Displacement

For known Snyk/SonarQube users (detected via Clay enrichment)

Subject: {{firstName}}, re: {{company}}'s Snyk setup
Hook: "Not bashing Snyk — they're solid for SCA. But if your team is still dealing with noisy SAST or missing PII in AI code, that's the gap we fill."
CTA: "Worth comparing results on one repo?"
